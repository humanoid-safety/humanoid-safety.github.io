Humanoid Robot Safety: Bridging Research, Industry Deployment, and Standards

IROS 2026 Workshop
Workshop Date: September 27, 2026

About the Workshop

Humanoid robots are rapidly transitioning from research prototypes to real-world deployment in logistics, manufacturing, and service environments. Unlike traditional industrial robots that operate within structured and fenced environments, humanoid robots are designed to work in unstructured spaces and interact closely with humans. These capabilities introduce new safety challenges that are not fully addressed by existing robotics safety frameworks.

This workshop aims to bring together researchers, industry practitioners, and safety engineers to discuss the emerging science and engineering of humanoid robot safety. The workshop will examine safety from multiple perspectives, including theoretical foundations in control and verification, safety-aware learning and autonomy, system-level safety engineering, and industrial functional safety practices.

The workshop will also discuss how existing robotics safety standards, such as ISO 10218 and ISO 13482, and emerging standards for dynamically stable robots (e.g., ISO 25785-1) may be extended to humanoid platforms.

A central objective of the workshop is to establish a shared language between the robotics research community and the safety engineering community. Through invited talks, panel discussions, and paper presentations, the workshop will explore key questions including: how safety theory can scale to high-dimensional humanoid systems, how learning-enabled autonomy can be deployed with safety guarantees, how industrial functional safety practices apply to humanoid robots, and what standards and benchmarks are needed for humanoid safety.

By bringing together experts from academia, robotics companies, and safety organizations, the workshop aims to identify key research and engineering challenges for building trustworthy humanoid robots that can safely operate alongside people.

Workshop Themes

Theme 1. Foundations of Safe Robot Autonomy

This theme explores theoretical approaches to safety in robotics, including safe control, control barrier functions, formal verification, runtime monitoring, and learning with safety guarantees. These methods provide mathematical tools for ensuring safety in robotic systems operating under uncertainty.

Theme 2. Safety in Humanoid Platforms

Humanoid robots introduce unique safety challenges due to dynamic locomotion, whole-body coordination, and contact-rich manipulation. This session focuses on ensuring safe locomotion, manipulation, and human–robot interaction in humanoid systems.

Theme 3. Functional Safety and Standards

Industrial deployment of robots requires compliance with safety standards and certification processes. This theme examines how existing functional safety frameworks, such as IEC 61508 and ISO robotics standards (e.g., ISO 10218 and ISO 13482), can be extended to humanoid robots and learning-enabled robotic systems.

Submission and Publication

  • We invite submissions of extended abstracts (up to 4 pages + references, optional appendix).
  • Submissions must follow the IEEE conference template with double-blind submission and review. Please refer to the Rules for the Double-Anonymous Review Process from IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (RAS).
  • The workshop is non-archival, meaning that submissions may also be under review at other venues. Previously released preprints, such as arXiv papers, are also permitted.
  • Submissions will be managed through OpenReview with link of.

Review Process

  • The review process is double-blind.
  • Each submission will receive at least two reviews from the program committee.
  • Submissions will be evaluated based on relevance, novelty/insight, clarity, technical quality, and discussion potential.

Presentation and Award

  • Accepted papers will be presented at the workshop poster session.
  • The workshop will recognize outstanding contributions through a Best Paper Award and a Runner-up Award.
  • The total award amount is USD 1,000, distributed between the two awards as cash prizes or commemorative souvenirs, sponsored by Siemens.
