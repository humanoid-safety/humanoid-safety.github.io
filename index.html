Humanoid Robot Safety: Bridging Research, Industry Deployment, and Standards

IROS 2026 Workshop on safe autonomy, verification, functional safety, standards, and deployment challenges for humanoid robots.

Overview

Humanoid robots are rapidly transitioning from research prototypes to real-world deployment in logistics, manufacturing, and service environments. Unlike traditional industrial robots, humanoids operate in unstructured environments, interact closely with humans, and rely on complex perception, learning, and control systems. These capabilities introduce new safety challenges that are not fully addressed by existing robotics safety frameworks.

This workshop brings together researchers, industry practitioners, and safety engineers to discuss the emerging science and engineering of humanoid robot safety. We focus on theoretical foundations in control and verification, safety-aware learning and autonomy, system-level safety engineering, and industrial functional safety practices, while also considering how existing and emerging standards can be extended to humanoid platforms.

A key goal of the workshop is to establish a shared language between the robotics research community and the safety engineering community.

Through invited talks, panel discussions, and paper presentations, the workshop will explore how safety theory can scale to high-dimensional humanoid systems, how learning-enabled autonomy can be deployed with safety guarantees, how industrial safety practices apply to humanoid robots, and what benchmarks and standards are needed for trustworthy deployment.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions on the emerging science and engineering of humanoid robot safety, spanning theory, systems, standards, and deployment.

Submission site: OpenReview (link to be added when available)

Topics of Interest

Foundations of Safe Robot Autonomy

  • Safe control and control barrier functions
  • Formal verification and runtime monitoring
  • Safe reinforcement learning and learning with guarantees
  • Safety certification for learning-enabled systems

Safety in Humanoid Platforms

  • Safe locomotion and balance control
  • Contact-rich manipulation safety
  • Whole-body control under safety constraints
  • Human–robot interaction safety

Functional Safety & Standards

  • Functional safety frameworks
  • Safety certification for robotics and AI systems
  • Risk assessment and hazard analysis for humanoid robots
  • Safety validation, benchmarking, and testing

Deployment and Industrial Perspectives

  • Safety engineering in humanoid robot products
  • Safety monitoring and fail-safe architectures
  • Safety assurance cases for autonomous systems
  • Industrial case studies and lessons learned

Submission Guidelines

  • Extended abstracts: <4 pages + references (optional appendix allowed)
  • Full submissions: 6–8 pages + references (optional appendix allowed)
  • Format: standard IEEE IROS conference template
  • Submission system: OpenReview
  • All submitting authors should maintain an up-to-date OpenReview profile

The workshop is non-archival, so submissions should generally not violate dual-submission policies at archival venues. Work currently under review elsewhere, or work that previously appeared on arXiv or in non-archival workshops, is welcome.

Review and Presentation Process

  • Review process: double-blind
  • Each submission receives at least two reviews
  • Meta-reviews provided by area chairs from the organizing team and senior researchers
  • Final decisions made jointly by the workshop organizers
  • Expected submissions: 30–50
  • Expected accepted papers: 15–20
  • All accepted papers are invited to the poster session
  • The top 2 papers will be selected for featured oral presentations and recognized as Best Paper and Runner-up

Awards will be determined based on review scores, technical quality, relevance to humanoid robot safety, and presentation quality during the workshop.
